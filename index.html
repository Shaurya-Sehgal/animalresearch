Woodland Caribou

By Shaurya

About them

The Woodland Caribou are animals that are currently threatened with only 3000 of them left. They are brown in colour and vary from 1-1.2 meters in height. The average weight for males is 180 kg and for females 135 kg. They can only be found in Yukon, Northwest Territories, British Columbia, Alberta, Saskatchewan, Manitoba, Ontario, Quebec, Newfoundland and Labrador and live in Canada's boreal forests. They require large contiguous areas of suitable habitat with low levels of disturbances which is why they live there. Their life span is around 15 years.

Why and What

So, you may be asking why are the woodland caribou threatened and how can we help? The woodland waribou are at risk due to human disturbance, construction of roads and pipelines, hunting, and habitat destruction. Forestry, agricultural spread, and mining have also degraded and fragmented the caribou's habitat. It also faces a few different challanges like a lack of food availability during extreme cold and ice and depression due to inbreeding. Whats being done? The Woodland Caribou is federally protected under the Species At Risk Act and under the Canada National Parks Act (where it is found in Canadian National Parks). It's also provincially protected in Ontario, Newfoundland and Labrador, the Northwest Territories, and Alberta.
